About Us

Nurwind Quartet

Nurwind Quartet is an ensemble dedicated to preserving and reimagining the ancient soundscapes of Armenia. Formed by four musicians who share a profound connection to the Armenian musical tradition, the group brings together the haunting voices of the duduk, blul, shvi, and zurna in a seamless dialogue between past and present.

Their music speaks across time — rooted in centuries-old folk traditions, yet alive with a contemporary sensitivity that resonates with audiences worldwide. From intimate concert halls to international stages, Nurwind Quartet carries the breath of Armenia wherever they travel.

Each member of the quartet brings decades of classical and folk training, having performed with leading ensembles across Armenia and abroad. Together, they form a singular voice — four musicians breathing as one, channeling the spirit of a people through the instruments they have carried for generations.

The Duduk

Armenia's Ancient Voice

The duduk is an ancient double-reed woodwind instrument made of apricot wood. It is indigenous to Armenia and traditionally played in pairs — a soloist and a steady drone called dam. The sound of two duduks together creates a richer texture that pierces directly into one's soul.

According to ethnomusicologist Dr. Jonathan McCollum, the instrument is depicted in numerous Armenian manuscripts of the Middle Ages, and is "actually the only truly Armenian instrument that has survived through history, and as such is a symbol of Armenian national identity." The most important quality of the duduk is its ability to express the language, dialectic, and mood of the Armenian people.

UNESCO proclaimed the art of the Armenian duduk and its music a Masterpiece of the Intangible Heritage of Humanity in 2005. Nurwind Quartet places the duduk at the center of their sound, surrounding it with complementary Armenian wind instruments to create a rich, resonant tapestry that honors its long history while exploring its contemporary possibilities.

Lessons

Duduk & Armenian Wind Instruments

Members of Nurwind Quartet offer private and group instruction in traditional Armenian wind instruments, including the duduk, blul, shvi, and zurna. Lessons are available both in person and online, and are designed for students of all levels — from the curious beginner to the advancing player seeking a deeper connection to the Armenian musical tradition.

The quartet's members have spent years teaching at leading Armenian state music colleges, bringing a wealth of pedagogical experience alongside their performing careers. Each lesson is approached with meticulous attention — every detail of breath, embouchure, tone, and phrasing is carefully addressed.

Lessons are conducted in Armenian, Russian, or English. For availability, scheduling, and rates, please reach out via the Contact page.
